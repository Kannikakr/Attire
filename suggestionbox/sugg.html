Books Suggestions

The conscious closet

The Revolutionary Guide to Looking Good While Doing Good by Elizabeth L. Cline Summary: This book is a comprehensive guide to building a sustainable wardrobe. Cline provides practical tips on how to choose ethical brands, shop secondhand, and extend the life of your clothes. She emphasizes the importance of mindful consumption and the power of individual choices in driving positive change in the fashion industry.


Loved cloths last

How to Make Your Wardrobe More Sustainable by Orsola de Castro Summary: De Castro encourages readers to rethink their relationship with clothing by focusing on repair, mending, and repurposing existing pieces. She provides step-by-step instructions and creative ideas for extending the lifespan of your wardrobe. The book challenges the fast-fashion culture and promotes a more sustainable and ethical approach to clothing consumption.


Slow Fashion

Aesthetics Meets Ethics by Safia Minney Summary: Minney, the founder of People Tree, explores the concept of slow fashion and its potential to transform the industry. She interviews designers, activists, and entrepreneurs who are working to create a more sustainable and equitable fashion system. The book delves into the social and environmental impacts of fast fashion and offers practical solutions for building a more sustainable wardrobe.

Slow Fashion

Fashion's Deadly Secrets by Lucy Siegle Summary: Siegle investigates the dark side of the fashion industry, exposing the environmental and social costs of fast fashion. Her book is a wake-up call to consumers and the fashion industry alike, urging us to demand change. Siegle highlights the negative impacts of textile production, labor exploitation, and waste generation, and provides insights into the ethical and sustainable alternatives available.
